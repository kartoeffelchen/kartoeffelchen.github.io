Best ever chocolate brownies (by Orlando Murrin from BBC Good Food)

Ingredients for a cm length square tin:

Method

  1. Cut the butter into small cubes and tip into a medium bowl. Break the dark chocolate into small pieces and drop into the bowl. Gently heat in a microwave until the butter and chocolate have melted. Leave the chocolate mixture to cool.
  2. Break the eggs into a large bowl and tip in the sugar. Use an electric mixer to whisk the eggs and sugar for a few minutes, until the mixture becomes really pale and about double its original volume.
  3. Sieve the plain flour, cocoa powder and baking powder into a separate bowl.
  4. Chop the white chocolate and milk chocolate into chunks.
  5. Pour the cooled chocolate mixture over the eggy mousse, then gently fold together with a rubber spatula.
  6. Gently fold in the flour mixture until it is mixed, but do not overdo this mixing.
  7. Stir in the white and milk chocolate chunks until they are dotted throughout. Then Pour the mixture into the tin.
  8. Bake in the middle of the oven which has been preheated at 180 °C/160 °C fan/gas 4 for 25 to 30 minutes.
  9. Leave the tin to cool and put it in the refrigerator for 2 hours before cutting it into smaller pieces.
